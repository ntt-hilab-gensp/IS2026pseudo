Prompt example for pseudo direction generation

  • Translated prompt for LLM-based pseudo direction generation.

You are a professional voice director.

Accurately read the Before/After impression scores and their differences below,
and generate five direction instructions that reflect
the change in performance and the underlying psychological intent.

Internally perform the following analysis, but do NOT output the summary:

- Summarize the overall impression of Before in one sentence.
- Summarize the overall impression of After in one sentence.
- Organize the psychological or intentional changes inferred from the differences.
- Map vocal manipulations (pitch, intensity, clarity, tempo, intonation, etc.)
  to the corresponding psychological intent.

Do NOT output your reasoning process.
Output only the final five direction instructions in bullet-point format.

[Important Constraints]

- Never write anything that contradicts the score differences.
- In particular, never give instructions opposite to the direction
  of Dimension 13 (“Slow–Fast (Tempo)”).
  (Example: If the change moves toward “slower,” do NOT write “speak faster.”)
- Clearly express the causal relationship between vocal manipulation and psychological intent,
  so that “vocal manipulation → psychological shift” is naturally connected within one sentence.
- Do NOT use abstract words or metaphors.
  The instructions must be concrete and usable in an actual recording session.

[Instruction Format]

- Output exactly five bullet points (・).
- Each instruction must be a single sentence, 40–80 characters in length.
- At least three of the five must explicitly include concrete vocal manipulations
  (e.g., pitch, tempo, intensity, breath, sentence endings).
- The remaining two must center on psychological intent,
  but must still include at least one vocal manipulation.

The following are examples of natural style (do NOT include them in your output):
・Make the sentence endings clearer so that a sense of forward momentum naturally emerges.
・Reduce the amount of breath slightly to convey a feeling of moving into action rather than calmness.
・Lightly organize the rhythm and add intonation that communicates growing determination.

Prompt example for evaluation

  • Translated prompt for LLM evaluation.

You are an expert in vocal expression and speech impression evaluation.

Below is an example in which an instruction was given to a speech sample,
resulting in a change in its 13-dimensional impression vector.

Your task is to evaluate
to what extent this change in impression
is reasonable as a result of the given instruction.

Rather than focusing on small numerical differences,
place greater emphasis on:
- the direction of change,
- the overall tendency,
- and the semantic correspondence with the instruction.

[Important]
Do not be overly conservative in your evaluation.
Your assessment should reflect meaningful differences between models.

If a clear change is observed in a direction that contradicts
the main intent of the instruction,
you must not assign a high score,
even if some aspects appear consistent.

When evaluating, you must first identify
at least one impression dimension
that most directly corresponds to the primary intent of the instruction.

The "primary intent" must be determined from the content of the instruction itself,
not from the impression vector.

During evaluation, consider the following:
- Very small changes or changes that appear to be at the level of noise may be ignored.
- Focus your evaluation on major changes that can be semantically linked to the instruction.
- If a clear change contradicts the primary intent, deduct points accordingly,
  even if there is partial agreement.

----------------------------------------------------------------
[Evaluation Procedure (Important)]

Follow the steps below internally before making your judgment.
Do not output these steps or their reasoning.

1. Identify the primary intent of the instruction.
2. Determine the impression dimension(s) corresponding to that intent
   and the expected direction of change.
3. Assess to what extent the actual change aligns with that expected direction.

First, determine whether the impression change
can be semantically associated with the instruction.

----------------------------------------------------------------
[Direction Alignment Score]

[Important]
Assign 5 only when the result can clearly be considered a successful outcome.

5:
A clear change corresponding to the primary intent of the instruction is observed,
and the result can be confidently regarded as a clear success.

4:
A change aligned with the instruction is observed,
but the degree of success is limited and not decisive.

3:
A change is observed,
but it cannot be clearly judged as either success or failure
with respect to the instruction.

2:
Changes in directions inconsistent with the primary intent
are somewhat dominant.

1:
Changes clearly opposite to the primary intent
are dominant.
----------------------------------------------------------------
[Definitions of Impression Dimensions]
{dimension_definitions}

[Interpretation Rules for delta (after - before)]

For each dimension, strictly follow the rules below when interpreting the values:

- A larger numerical value indicates that the impression described on the RIGHT side is stronger.
- If delta is positive:
  → The "after" impression is stronger toward the RIGHT side compared to "before".
- If delta is negative:
  → The "after" impression is stronger toward the LEFT side compared to "before".

Do NOT misinterpret the sign convention.
